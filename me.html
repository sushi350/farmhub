Climate change

Climate change is known to be a common issue all around the world. Mainly, in Africa, the causes would be deforestation, bad farming habits, some human activities and the emission of greenhouse gas. This will lead to change in the ecosystem and even extreme weather events. The effects of that would be animal extinctions, decrease in resources, no stability, drought...etc. We humans need to take action to reduce the impact. Lets protect today, the coming generation and even after.

About FarmBud

What is FarmBud

Climate change is an issue affecting all parts of the world. Mainly in Africa, the main causes would be deforestation, unsuitable agricultural practices and increasing industrialization. FARMBUD is an application built to benefit both the communication of people and the well-being of the environment. How? You may ask.

How it works

FARMBUD has a unique way of working. The app studies the weather usually existing in a specific environment. Using that information, it provides options of plants suitable for that specific environment. To make things a little bit more interesting it has a feature that tells you some fun facts about a plant and even some ancient myths. Once the user has decided on a specific plant of his/her interest, it matches with a bunch of other people having the same preference of plants as well as botanists who currently have the species available for sale. The app will then schedule a time and date for these people to meet and work on planting of the seeds.

Purpose of FarmBud

As you have read earlier this app is supposed to bring people together and contribute to the environment. This will be a more in-depth explanation to how it will do so. There are many factors that affect climate change. Usually, in the urban areas, deforestation is the problem. This app will clearly help increase afforestation for urban areas but how about the rural areas? The causes of climate change for rural areas are mostly overgrazing, fertilizers and pesticides. FARMBUD will not only help in growing plants in urban areas, but it will also help in creating awareness which would lead to donations. These donations could be used on fertilizers and pesticides that have more quality which would mean less risk to climate change.
